The following is a map of surface nitrate concentrations.

Light blue colors are low nitrate concentrations, and green colors are high concentrations. Superimposed on the image are circles for where researchers went to sample the ocean. At each point they took a sample of seawater and added different nutrients to see which would make the phytoplankton grow quickest. This is a way to determine which nutrient is currently in shortest supply relative to what the plankton need. This is known as the limiting nutrient, a nutrient which limits te growth of phytoplankton even if others are in high-supply.

There are typically two limiting nutrients in the ocean - Nitrogen (in the form of Nitrate NO_32-) and Iron. The locations where phytoplankton are iron limited are shown with red dots and those that are nitrogen limited are shown with green dots.

  • Q: How do the distribution of red and green dots map to the underlying distribution of nitrate? Does this make sense?
