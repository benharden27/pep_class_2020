Physical Oceanography is the study of how water moves. This includes ocean currents, waves and large scale ocean circulation. Some of the most fundimental ideas revolve around the key physical properties of the water: Temperature, Salinity and Density. We will explore these properties and how they allow or limit movement of water in the ocean.

1 Reading

Read the following from Segar:

  • Chapter 1: Introduction (pg. 1-2); The Oceans and Earth’s Environment (pg. 2-6). These sections are less explicitly about physical oceanography, but provide a good overview and motivation for studying the ocean.
  • Chapter 7: + Climate and Ocean Surface Water Properties (pg. Water and Heat Budgets (pg. 147-149). This covers the input and removal of heat and water from the ocean.
  • Critical Concepts 1 and 6. These cover the key ideas that relate temperature and salinity (and pressure) to density
